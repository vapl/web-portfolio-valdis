<Section id="problem" title="Problem">
  NFT markets are highly exposed to manipulation patterns such as rug pulls,
  wash trading, and whale-driven price movements. While blockchain data is
  transparent, identifying these patterns requires technical knowledge and
  manual investigation.

  <PullQuote>
    "We turn raw on-chain noise into actionable alerts."
  </PullQuote>
</Section>

<Section id="scope" title="Scope & Responsibilities">
  NFTs Guard was developed as a solo project, covering the entire product
  lifecycle from concept definition to a production-ready MVP.

  <ul>
    <li>- Defining the product concept and MVP scope</li>
    <li>- UX/UI and interaction design</li>
    <li>- Frontend development using Next.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS</li>
    <li>- Backend logic and database design with Supabase and PostgreSQL</li>
    <li>- Integrating Ethereum blockchain data as an external data source</li>
    <li>- Designing heuristic-based risk detection and scoring logic</li>
    <li>- Performance optimization and system stability</li>
  </ul>
</Section>

<Section id="challenges" title="Technical Challenges">
  One of the main challenges was working with raw blockchain data in a traditional
  Web2 architecture. While the data is public, it is noisy, inconsistent, and not
  designed for direct user consumption.

  <ul>
    <li>- Normalizing and interpreting noisy on-chain data</li>
    <li>- Designing a readable risk score for non-technical users</li>
    <li>- Maintaining low latency while relying on external data sources</li>
    <li>- Avoiding tight coupling between blockchain logic and UI components</li>
  </ul>
</Section>

<Section id="decisions" title="MVP Decisions & Trade-offs">
  To keep the project focused and achievable, several conscious trade-offs were
  made during development. Rather than building decentralized execution logic,
  the platform treats blockchain strictly as a data layer.

  <ul>
    <li>- Web2-first architecture with blockchain as a data source</li>
    <li>- Ethereum-only support for faster validation</li>
    <li>- Heuristic-based risk flags instead of ML-heavy solutions</li>
    <li>- Scalable architecture without premature complexity</li>
  </ul>
</Section>
